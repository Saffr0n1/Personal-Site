About

I am a freshman at Harvard University studying Mathematics, Physics, and Computer Science. I graduated from Hunter College High School in 2018.

My primary interests lie within the intersection of mathemsatics and computer science with challenges in quantum networks, solar energy, and finance. I am currently working in the laboratory of Professor Marko Lončar at Harvard's John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, where I am developing a model of silicon vacancy (SiV) centers in diamond. My previous work has involved research in organic polymer solar cells at Stony Brook University and in mucosal immunology at Rockefeller University.

My skills include Python (for data science and ML), Java, JS, Bootstrap, HTML5, CSS, and MatLab

In my free time, I love writing, reading novels and memoirs, and playing soccer.
